Health · Workout Routine
Push · Pull · Rehab
Every other day · 30–35 min · Day A alternates with Day B · 1 free skip per week · no penalty
Push + Core · ~30 min
01
Push-ups
3 sets · build toward failure · 60–90s rest between sets
Start with whatever you can do with good form — currently ~30. Hands shoulder-width, body in a straight line from head to heels. Lower until chest nearly touches the floor, elbows at ~45° from body (not flared out wide). Full extension at the top but don't lock elbows. When you can hit 40+ reps in set 1 consistently, add a fourth set or try diamond push-ups.
Push
02
OYO Nova — Chest Fly or Shoulder Press
2 sets · 12–15 reps · pick one per session
Chest fly: Stand on the band, handles at sides, slight forward lean. Bring handles together in front of your chest in a wide arc, slight bend in elbows throughout. Slow on the way back out — that's the work.

Shoulder press: Stand on band, handles at shoulder height, press straight overhead. Core tight, don't arch your lower back. Alternate between sessions to hit different angles.
Push
Alternate chest fly and shoulder press session to session — keeps it fresh and hits different angles.
03
Lying Leg Raises
3 sets · 10–15 reps · slow and controlled
Lie flat on your back, hands under your lower back for support (palms down). Legs straight, raise them to ~90° then lower slowly — stop just before heels touch the floor. The lowering phase is where the core work happens. Keep your lower back pressed into the floor the whole time. If 15 reps gets easy, add a 4th set or hold a slight pause at the bottom.
Core
04
Plank
2 holds · 30–45 sec · build to 60 sec over weeks
Forearms on the floor, elbows under shoulders, body in a straight line. Squeeze your glutes and core — don't let your hips sag or pike up. Breathe steadily. If 45 sec is easy, extend to 60 sec before adding a third set. Quality over duration — a 30-second perfect plank beats a 60-second sagging one.
Core
Pull + Rehab · ~35 min
01
Pull-ups (or Cable Rows / OYO Rows)
3 sets · as many as possible · 90s rest
Pull-ups (once bar is up): Dead hang start, pull until chin clears the bar. Squeeze shoulder blades together as you pull — don't just yank with arms. Lower slowly. Even 3–4 reps per set is excellent starting out.

Cable rows (gym): Seated, cable at chest height, pull to your lower chest with elbows close to body. Pause at peak contraction, slow release.

OYO rows (home, no bar yet): Anchor band to a door handle, lean back, row handles to your sides. Keep core engaged.
Pull
02
OYO Nova — Bicep Curl
2 sets · 12–15 reps each arm
Stand on the band, handles at sides, palms facing forward. Curl both handles to shoulder height — keep elbows pinned to your sides, don't let them drift forward. Squeeze at the top, slow on the way down. Can alternate arms for better mind-muscle connection.
Pull
03
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
3 sets · 8 reps each leg · bodyweight · very slow
Stand on one leg, slight bend in the standing knee. Hinge at the hip — let your torso come forward and the free leg extend behind you as a counterbalance. Go until you feel a stretch in the hamstring of the standing leg, then drive through the hip to return upright. Keep your back flat throughout — no rounding. Go slow: 3 seconds down, 1 second hold, 2 seconds up. This is your hip tendon rehab exercise — the hip on the standing leg is doing the work.
Rehab
⚠ Right hip — this is the target side. If you feel sharp pain (not muscle burn) stop immediately. Mild discomfort/tension in the hip tendon area is normal and expected. Start with bodyweight only, no added load.
04
Dead Bug
2 sets · 10 reps each side · controlled
Lie on your back, arms pointing straight up at the ceiling, knees bent at 90° in the air (like an upside-down bug). The key: press your lower back FLAT into the floor and keep it there the whole time. Slowly lower your right arm behind your head and left leg toward the floor simultaneously — stop just before they touch. Return to start, switch sides. If your lower back lifts off the floor, you've gone too far. Start small.
Core
Dead bug looks deceptively easy. The core engagement needed to keep your lower back flat is the entire point — you'll feel it by rep 6.

Progression — first 3 weeks
Week 1 (Jun 15–21)
Push-ups: 3 × ~30 → establish baseline
SLRDL: bodyweight only, focus on form and balance
Dead bug: small range of motion, get the back-flat feeling
Plank: 2 × 30 sec
Week 2 (Jun 22–28)
Push-ups: push for 35+ in set 1
SLRDL: increase range of motion if hip feels good
Dead bug: full range, both sides
Plank: 2 × 40 sec
Week 3 (Jun 29–Jul 5)
Push-ups: if hitting 40+ reps, add a 4th set or try diamond push-ups
SLRDL: add 1kg hand weight if feeling strong (can of food works)
Pull-ups: if bar is installed, attempt first reps — any is great
Plank: 2 × 45–60 sec
Hip tendon guidelines
⚠ What to avoid
Explosive movements on the right hip — no sprinting or hard kicking until you've built 4+ weeks of consistent SLRDL. The tendon responds to progressive load, not rest. Gradual is the whole point.
Pickup soccer
Fine to play — warm up the hip properly first (leg swings, hip circles, light jogging). Avoid maximal effort kicks for the first 2 weeks. By week 3 if SLRDL is going well, moderate kicks are fine.
What "rehab" actually means here
Hip tendon issues respond best to eccentric and isometric loading — slow, controlled movements that stress the tendon just enough to promote adaptation. The SLRDL does exactly this. Pain during (mild tension = ok, sharp = stop) and 24–48hr soreness after are both normal. Ongoing pain at rest is not — that's the signal to back off.
OYO Nova tips
The resistance increases as you extend the cables — so the hardest part of each rep is at full extension. This is actually ideal for most exercises. For curls, stand slightly wider on the band to increase base resistance. For rows, the further you lean back, the harder it gets.
When equipment arrives
Pull-up bar installed
Start with negatives if you can't do a full pull-up yet: jump to the top position, lower yourself as slowly as possible (aim for 5 seconds). 3 × 3–5 negatives builds the strength fast. Within 2–3 weeks of consistent negatives, full pull-ups follow.
Planet Fitness
Cable machine replaces OYO rows with better resistance curve. Lat pulldown machine is a good pull-up progression tool. Don't overthink the gym — the routine above works there too, just swap OYO for cables.
